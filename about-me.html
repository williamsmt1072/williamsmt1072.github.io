About me

My name is Mitchell Williams and I have been living in Riverton, Utah for the past 20 years. I was born in Murray, Utah but I moved to southern California shortly after my birth where my father completed his medical residency. After relocating yet again, Riverton has been my home for the last two decades. During my sophomore year of High School, I attended Riverton High School until I then decided to try something more challenging and moved to Itineris Early College High School. At Itineris, I enrolled in college-level classes and worked diligently towards obtaining my associate's degree. I am proud to say that I achieved this goal as I graduated with my associate's degree and am now pursuing further education. After completing my associate of science degree from Salt Lake Community College in the summer following my high school graduation, I decided to transfer to the University of Utah. The University of Utah seemed like a perfect fit for me as it offered a great program for my degree of choice. I am currently working towards my bachelor's degree in Biomedical Engineering with a biomechanical emphasis. I have grown to love the academic environment at the University of Utah and am inspired by the innovation and research that is taking place here. Vortex Design began as a freelance graphic and web design business in the beginning of 2020, during the time when the COVID-19 pandemic started. Initially, I was making posters and banners for local businesses. However, with time, the business began to expand and I started taking on more contracting work from small businesses and medical organizations. In addition to running Vortex Design, I also work full-time as a medical assistant for Foot and Ankle Specialists, PLLC. Working in healthcare is an entirely different experience for me, but I have been able to bring some of my creative skills into this role as well.
